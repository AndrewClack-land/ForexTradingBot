Method: 10,000 bootstrap paths with replacement, horizon = 51 trades (same as current sample). Equity in *R* units (1R = risk per trade). Drawdown computed on cumulative R curve.

## Interpretation

- With the current win-rate (≈21.6%) and fixed 1:1.5 RR profile, the distribution leans heavily negative. Every simulated path finished down, averaging −23.6R after 51 trades.
- Even the “best” decile of simulations still lost ≈13.5R, so the sample implies a negative expectancy until we either raise win-rate, increase reward per winner, or reduce losers.
- Risk tolerance: to survive the 90th percentile drawdown (≈33R), capital should be sized to stomach ~33 consecutive losses of 1R each.
